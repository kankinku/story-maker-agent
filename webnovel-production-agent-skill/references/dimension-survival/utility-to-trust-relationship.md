# Utility To Trust Relationship

Use this reference for companion relationships that begin as practical survival utility and grow into emotional trust.

## Relationship Arc

```text
transaction for mutual survival
-> guidance and technical support
-> small arguments and distinct reactions
-> repeated joint problem-solving
-> recognition of each other's competence
-> one side accepts cost for the other
-> protagonist violates a survival rule to choose the companion
-> relationship becomes part of the story engine
```

Emotion is proven when the protagonist breaks or bends a practical survival rule for the companion. Do not announce intimacy before the relationship has altered behavior.

## Rule

```yaml
id: utility-to-trust
stages:
  - The companion gives practical utility.
  - The protagonist solves one of the companion's deficits.
  - Shared work exposes small personality markers.
  - The companion accepts a cost.
  - The protagonist chooses the companion despite time, resource, or survival loss.
avoid:
  - Immediate unconditional trust after first meeting.
  - Declaring closeness only through dialogue.
  - Making the companion a perfect answer machine.
verify:
  - Does the companion increase options without replacing protagonist agency?
  - Does the protagonist make the final decision?
  - Does the relationship create cost as well as utility?
```

## Companion Role Limits

The expert companion may:

- explain mechanisms
- propose routes
- expose incomplete predictions
- repair or translate gear
- challenge a decision

The expert companion must not:

- choose all routes
- identify all weaknesses
- select all equipment
- solve all fights
- remove risk from unknown dimensions

## Home-World Emotional Anchor

The home world must not become a lobby screen.

Return scenes should do at least two:

1. prove the value of acquired resources
2. change living conditions
3. reveal a new bottleneck
4. advance an old relationship or home-world problem
5. set the next expedition objective

Past relationships, promises, and losses give personal meaning to otherwise practical farming.
